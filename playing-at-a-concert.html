Playing a Concert

If you hit a wrong note or make a mistake, don't stop playing - just carry on and look as though you mean it!

Playing a Concert can be great fun! Or it can be totally scary! Sometimes both at the same time...

So we have put together a list of our top tips for great concerts.

Copy Out a Set-List

A set-list is a list of all your songs in the order you will play them in.

Make copies for everyone!

Print BIG! so that you can still read it if it's down by your feet on the floor or if the lighting isn't very good.

Remember your spares

Some intruments have parts that need to be replaced if they wear out or break. For example:

  • guitar strings
  • saxaphone or clarinet reeds
  • drumsticks

Make a list of the spares you might need and make sure you kno where they are in an emergency.

Plan where you will be on the stage

Before you start to set up your instruments, take a few minutes to decide where you're all going to be.

  • Will the drums be on centre stage? Or off to the side?
  • Is there a power-socket for the amplifier if your guitar player stands on the right?

It's much better to think about all this before you start setting up your intruments. It's really annoying if you have to start unplugging things and swapping things places once you've begun to set things up!

Know where you're going, know when you're on

If you're playing a concert somewhere you've never been before make sure you know where it is. Print out a map or set the sat-nav before you set out. There's nothing worse than being lost in an unfamiliar place half-an-hour before you're due to go onstage.

And when you do arrive, the first thing you should do is find out what time you're on. On the night, times often change. Ask whoever is running the event when you'll be on. Dont get caught out!
